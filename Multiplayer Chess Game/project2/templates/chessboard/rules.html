  • Pawn: Advances one square, although from its initial position, it can advance two squares, captures in a diagonal fashion..
  • Rook: Can move in any number of squares both vertically and horizontally. It has a role in casting as well.
  • Knight: Forms a L shape by moving two squares in one direction and then one square in the opposite direction. The ability to leap over other pieces is unique to it.
  • Bishop: Can move any number of squares diagonally. With one bishop on a light square and one on a dark square, each player begins with two bishops.
  • Queen: Can move any number of squares in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal direction. On the board, it is the most potent piece.
  • King: Can move in any direction by one square. The aim of the game is to checkmate the king of the opposition.
